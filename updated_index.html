What is ZotBins?



We are an independent group of undergraduate researchers, called the ZerO Waste Anteaters (ZOWA), who are passionate about technology-based zero waste management. We are recruiting volunteers who want to work on our zero waste project called ZotBins and make a difference for the environment. ZotBins is a smart waste bin system that collects data to help make waste management more efficient and to promote zero waste.

Subgroups


  • Web and Mobile
  • Waste Recognition
  • Embedded System
  • Hardware and Electrical


Web and Mobile


Our web and mobile engineers are dedicated to ensuring ZotBins is accessible to our end users. This team is responsible for building the ZotBins web and mobile app: the web app provides functionality to help facilities management in cultivating a sustainable campus, while the mobile app seeks to engage users in more environmentally-conscious practices. Members of this group are comfortable working in the front and back ends, and have experience with UI/UX design. Common tasks include designing aesthetically-pleasing user interfaces, web development, and app development (Android and iOS).



Waste Recognition


The waste recognition team is focused on researching different opportunities in computationally recognizing waste. Automatic waste detection has various benefits such as giving proper waste disposal instructions to students. We are currently focused on two main projects: classifying waste by image through deep learning methods and identifying waste products given their barcode number. This subgroup is the most recently formed team, so we are looking for motivated individuals who are willing to build from scratch and become future leaders.



Embedded System


The embedded systems team enables the Smart Bin’s sensors and microcontrollers to work together in order to reliably collect, manage, and transfer data. Members of this group generally work on writing software that can handle the flow of data robustly, safely, and cleanly. Members of this group are comfortable with general IoT concepts, so are also comfortable working with making changes to the Smart Bin’s digital signage. Common tasks include developing and debugging software targeted for a microcontroller, writing code that sends data via API calls, and writing API endpoints.



Hardware and Electrical


We depend on the hardware team to build our physical Smart Bins. The hardware and electrical engineers work with Raspberry Pis, sensors, and PCBs to design robust, cost-efficient prototypes capable of collecting waste weight, bin fullness, and other useful metrics. Common tasks include 3D modeling and printing, circuit and PCB design, power draw analytics, and manufacturing bin prototypes.

Our Team


ZotBins is organized by subgroups-- each with a subgroup lead and various subgroup members. Overall, all of the subgroups are led by team leads. Although we are an undergraduate research group and participate in research, we are primarily focused on giving each of our team members technical, project-based working experiences.
